Indira Gandhi- Prime Minister of India

Main Facts

Indira Priyadarshini Gandhi was born in 1917 in Allahabad, India. She was the Prime Minister of India. She became Prime Minister in 1966 and is well known for many things including agricultural improvements. She also was very successful in the Pakistan War which led to the formation of the country of Bangladesh in 1971. She served three terms, but was not reelected for a fourth term by of her increasing power. Shortly after not being reelected for a 4th term she was assassinated in 1984 by her bodyguards. Childhood

Growing up Indira attended many schools and was very intelligent. Her father, the prime minister, was absent often because of his imprisonment because he was a leader of the India Independence Movement. Her mother also died of tuberculosis in 1963 so she lived with family friends. After her father's death she was appointed Prime Minister of India.

Legacy

Indira Gandhi is both admired and hated by many. Though she still remains the only female Prime Minister of India. She always showed compassion to the poor and helped grow India into a powerful country. Many thought she had to much power, but no one can deny that she is a big part of Indian history and a powerful woman leader who deserves to be remembered.

What did she Accomplish?

Indira accomplished many things as Prime Minister. Indira settled disputes between Pakistan during the war which led to the formation of India. She also led India into the nuclear age. Many thought that she was to authoritative and had to much power which ultimately led to her not being reelected. She was in prison for corruption, but after getting out she managed to become prime minister again in 1980. After being killed in 1984 after the attack of the Golden Temple her son immediately took over as Prime Minister.
